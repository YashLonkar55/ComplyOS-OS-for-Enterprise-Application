# Understanding the Enterprise

Imagine you're **Yamaha**, **Kodak**, or **Verkada**—companies like those mentioned by Pax.

You don't have just one software system.

You have **20+ different systems** working across the organization.

Examples include:

- SAP ERP
- Oracle ERP
- Salesforce CRM
- Excel Sheets
- Emails
- Google Drive
- SharePoint
- Internal Dashboards
- Government Portals
- PDF Documents
- CSV Files
- Warehouse Systems
- Finance Software

Every department uses something different.

Now imagine you're the **Compliance Officer**.

Your job is to answer one simple question:

> **"Are we compliant?"**

The information you need is spread across all of these systems.

---

# The Enterprise Problem

Think about where different pieces of information live:

- Invoice → SAP
- Shipment → Warehouse System
- Customs Entry → Government Portal
- Commercial Invoice → PDF
- Email Approval → Outlook
- Compliance Rules → Government Website

None of these systems are naturally connected.

As a result, **humans become the integration layer**.

They manually:

- Copy information between systems
- Compare documents
- Verify compliance
- Prepare reports

This is exactly where AI becomes valuable.

## Key Insight

Notice something important:

- We are **not replacing SAP**.
- We are **not replacing Oracle**.
- We are **adding intelligence on top of existing enterprise systems**.

This is how many successful enterprise AI companies position themselves.
